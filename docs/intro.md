# Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

Welcome to the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics textbook! This comprehensive guide is designed to introduce you to the fascinating world of Physical AI - AI systems that interact with and operate in the physical world.

## About This Textbook

This textbook focuses on AI systems operating in the physical world, bridging the gap between digital intelligence and embodied robotic systems. It's designed to support Panaversity's vision of AI-native education and prepares learners for future roles where humans, AI agents, and robots collaborate.

## Who Should Read This

This textbook is designed for:
- Undergraduate and early graduate students in Computer Science and AI
- Software developers transitioning from digital AI to robotics
- Beginners in Physical AI and humanoid robotics

No prior robotics hardware experience is assumed.

## What You'll Learn

The textbook is organized into four core modules:

1. **Physical AI Fundamentals**: Understanding the theoretical foundations that bridge digital AI with physical-world robotics
2. **ROS 2 as Robotic Nervous System**: How ROS 2 functions conceptually without getting lost in technical implementation details
3. **Simulation and AI Integration**: Understanding simulation, digital twins, and NVIDIA Isaac integration for AI-powered perception
4. **Vision-Language-Action Systems**: How VLA systems enable natural human-robot interaction

## How to Use This Textbook

Each module builds upon the previous one, providing a clear progression from fundamentals to advanced concepts. The textbook uses clear, simple English with minimal mathematics, focusing on conceptual understanding rather than implementation details.

Key features include:
- Learning objectives at the beginning of each section
- Key takeaways to summarize important concepts
- Practice questions to test your understanding
- Examples, diagrams, and analogies to clarify complex concepts

## Technology Used

This textbook is built with Docusaurus, making it accessible across devices and searchable. Equations are rendered using MathJax, and diagrams are created with Mermaid.

## Our Approach

This textbook follows a concept-first approach, focusing on understanding before implementation. We emphasize intuitive explanations over mathematical complexity, using analogies and visual elements to clarify difficult concepts. This approach ensures that readers can grasp the fundamental ideas before diving into technical specifics.

Each module is designed to be self-contained while building upon previous knowledge. You can read them sequentially for a comprehensive understanding, or select individual modules based on your interests or curriculum requirements.
